1. Visit JLCPCB uploader
    - [ ]  Consider your board type, colour, finish and check manufacturing specs
2. Make your schematic
3. Perform Electrical Rules Check (ERC)
4. Draw your board outline
    - [ ]  This happens on the "edge cuts" layer
5. Assign footprints to components
    - [ ]  Decide on through-hole or surface mount
    - [ ]  Perform physical component check (calipers/datasheets)
    - [ ]  Update the board to populate the design with your components
6. Layout the PCB
    - [ ]  Place your components on front and/or back
    - [ ]  Perform Design Rules check (DRC) to identify conflicts early
7. Route traces
    - [ ]  Decide on track widths
    - [ ]  Make ground/power planes
    - [ ]  Systematically route the "rats-nest"
    - [ ]  Perform the DRC check (yes, again!)
8. Export, Upload and Check
    - [ ]  Install the “Fabrication Toolkit” plugin
    - [ ]  Use it to export a production .zip file (Gerber/Drill Files)
    - [ ]  Upload to JLCPCB, perform Design For Manufacture (DFM) check (jlcdfm.com) & correct
9. Add silkscreening and graphics
    - [ ]  Draw/import graphics using PCB as your palette (board, mask, plated copper, silkscreen)
    - [ ]  Use 3D Viewer for final visual check
    - [ ]  Export/Upload to JLCPCB and perform DFM once again
